The TikTok Ban Debate – Explained

Summary

Congress is debating a nationwide ban of TikTok, citing national security threats tied to its Chinese ownership. Supporters see it as necessary; critics call it censorship.

Background

TikTok is owned by ByteDance, a Chinese tech firm. U.S. officials worry the app could share user data with China’s government or influence public opinion.

Viewpoints

Supporters: Say it’s a national security risk and must be banned.

Opponents: Say there's no hard evidence, and banning it limits free speech and hurts creators.

Why It Matters

This case represents a bigger question about internet regulation, tech control, and the role of government in protecting (or limiting) digital freedom.
